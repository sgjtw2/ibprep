Final Assessment
Scored as a GT Corporate Finance interviewer would score you. Honest. No grade inflation.
Top 3 Strengths
Valuation analytical depth — you can build and defend a comparable company analysis, EBITDA normalization, and scenario model with real deal context behind it.
Client-facing credibility — four years presenting investment recommendations to HNWI founders with real capital at stake. This is rare at SA/AM level.
CAIA + CFA L2 credentials signal genuine commitment to investment and corporate finance, not a lateral from a non-financial background.
Top 3 Weaknesses
No transaction execution experience — no live deal closed, no IM drafted in a transactional context, no data room managed. This is the primary objection.
Precedent transaction analysis is conceptual only — you cannot name specific deals and walk through the multiple derivation the way a direct M&A hire can.
Capital raising is a gap — GT CF includes equity capital markets and fundraising advisory; you have no experience on the origination or execution side of raising capital for a client.
Likely Interviewer Concerns
Can he actually close a transaction, or will he need 12 months before he can contribute independently to deal execution?
His deal experience is self-directed analysis, not supervised M&A work — how much of the valuation framework is solid versus situationally learned?
Wealth management background means he's trained to manage client expectations, not to drive a process to completion on a deadline. Will he adapt quickly?
Psychology and Communication degree — no accounting or finance undergraduate foundation. Can he speak to audited financials, tax structuring, and deal mechanics credibly?
